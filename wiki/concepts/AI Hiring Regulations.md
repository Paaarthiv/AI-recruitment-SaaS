# AI Hiring Regulations

## Definition

The growing body of laws, guidelines, and frameworks governing the use of AI in employment decisions. These regulations typically address bias, transparency, explainability, and candidate rights when AI systems are used to screen, rank, or evaluate job applicants.

## Relevance to AI Recruitment

Compliance is emerging as a **competitive differentiator**, not just a cost. Key regulatory landscape:

### EU AI Act
- Classifies AI hiring systems as **high-risk** — requires conformity assessments, bias audits, transparency, and human oversight
- Affects any company operating in or selling to EU markets
- Most comprehensive AI regulation globally

### NYC Local Law 144 (USA)
- Requires annual **bias audits** for automated employment decision tools (AEDTs) used in NYC
- First US law specifically targeting AI hiring tools
- Set a precedent for other jurisdictions

### EEOC Guidance (USA)
- US Equal Employment Opportunity Commission guidance on AI and algorithmic discrimination
- Applies existing anti-discrimination law (Title VII, ADA) to AI-driven hiring decisions
- Emphasizes **disparate impact** analysis
