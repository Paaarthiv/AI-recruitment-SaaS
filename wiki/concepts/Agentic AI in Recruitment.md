# Agentic AI in Recruitment

## Definition

**Agentic AI** refers to autonomous AI systems capable of handling multi-step workflows end-to-end — not just responding to individual queries, but planning, executing, and adapting across a sequence of tasks. In recruitment, this means AI agents that can independently source candidates, screen applications, schedule interviews, send follow-ups, and make recommendations — with human oversight rather than human orchestration.

## Relevance to AI Recruitment

This is the **defining technology trend of 2025** in the AI recruitment space. The market is shifting from:
- **Chatbots** (scripted, single-turn interactions) → **Agents** (autonomous, multi-step workflows)
- **Tool augmentation** (AI helps humans do their jobs) → **Task automation** (AI does entire tasks independently)

Companies building agentic capabilities ([[Paradox]], [[Alex.com]], [[Mercor]]) are positioned at the frontier of where the market is heading.

## Current State

- [[Paradox]]'s "Olivia" is the most mature agent-like system, handling scheduling and screening autonomously
- [[Alex.com]] and [[Mercor]] are emerging players building fully autonomous interview experiences
- Most incumbents ([[Greenhouse]], [[Workable]]) still offer AI as a feature layer, not an agent architecture
- The gap between "AI-enhanced" and "AI-agent" products is widening

## Key Players

- [[Paradox]] — Leading in conversational agent workflows
- [[Alex.com]] — Autonomous interview agent
