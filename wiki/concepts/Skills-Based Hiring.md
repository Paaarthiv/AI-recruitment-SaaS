# Skills-Based Hiring

## Definition

**Skills-based hiring** is a recruitment methodology that evaluates candidates based on demonstrated skills and inferred potential rather than traditional credentials (degrees, job titles, years of experience). AI enables this by analyzing work samples, projects, career trajectories, and behavioral signals to predict job performance.

## Relevance to AI Recruitment

This is a major market shift driving demand for AI recruitment tools:
- Companies are dropping degree requirements (Google, Apple, IBM led this trend)
- AI can infer skills from non-obvious signals (GitHub repos, portfolio projects, career patterns)
- Enables better diversity outcomes by reducing credential bias
- Creates demand for sophisticated matching algorithms — a core value proposition for AI platforms

## Current State

- [[Eightfold AI]] has the most sophisticated skills-based matching (deep learning + skills ontology)
- [[Findem]] uses attribute-based search as a form of skills inference
- Traditional ATS platforms ([[Greenhouse]], [[Workable]]) still primarily use keyword-based matching
- Open skills frameworks exist (O*NET, ESCO, Lightcast) but no standard has won

## Key Players

- [[Eightfold AI]] — Enterprise skills ontology and talent intelligence
- [[Findem]] — Attribute-based people search
- [[SeekOut]] — Sourcing with skills and diversity filters
- [[Workable]] — SMB/mid-market ATS with AI sourcing features

## Our Position

**Core differentiator opportunity.** If we build superior skills inference and matching, we can compete with keyword-based ATS tools on quality of hire. LLMs may unlock new approaches to skills extraction from unstructured data (portfolios, cover letters, project descriptions).
